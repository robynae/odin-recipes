Lasagna Recipe

Perfection in the form of pasta!

This is probably the best recipe that I make. I only make it for special occasions because it's a real labor of love! It includes a homemade bolognese sauce, a mornay sauce, homemade pasta, and a few more layers of awesome deliciousness. Read on for a recipe that's sure to be a real showstopper.

Ingredients

For the pasta:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 5 eggs
  • A pinch of salt

For the bolognese:

  • 1 1/2 lbs of ground beef
  • 1 1/2 lbs of ground pork
  • 8 strips of bacon or pancetta, cut into lardons
  • 1 large onion, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and finely chopped
  • 3 ribs celery, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup tomato paste
  • 1-2 cups Sauvignon Blanc
  • 1 can crushed San Marzano tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Parmesan cheese rind
  • Olive oil
  • Italian seasoning
  • Salt
  • Pepper

For the mornay sauce:

  • 4 tbsps butter
  • 4 tbsps all-purpose flour
  • 8 oz grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 full head of garlic, peeled and minced
  • Salt
  • Pepper

Extras:

  • 1 package of prosciutto
  • 1 package of fresh mazzarella cheese- the sliced kind
  • 1 package of fresh basil
  • 8 oz of not fresh mozzarella, shredded

Cooking Instructions:

For the pasta:

  1. Make a pile of flour and salt on your clean counter-top, create a well in the middle and crack in your eggs
  2. Beat the eggs in the middle, incorporating a small amount of flour at a time until a shaggy dough forms
  3. Knead the dough by hand until smooth
  4. Once smooth, wrap in plastic wrap and let rest for about 20 minutes
  5. Use your pasta maker to roll out your pasta little by little until you can see your hand through it
  6. Cut pasta into strips
  7. Bring water seasoned with a generous amount of salt to a boil
  8. Cooking in batches, boil your noodles for aboout 30 seconds at a time, or until they puff up and change color
  9. Set aside until assembly. Do NOT set the pasta on top of each other, make sure you have plenty of counter space

For the bolognese:

  1. Cook the bacon, set aside
  2. Brown the beef and pork in the same pot as the bacon, set aside
  3. Add in your vegetables, cook until softened, add in the bacon
  4. Add in your tomato paste, allow to caramelize
  5. Once tomato paste is caramelized, add in your white wine. Stir until the alcohol is cooked off
  6. Ad back your meats
  7. Cover with your can of San Marzano tomatoes, stirring with a wooden spoon to further crush the tomatoes
  8. Add in your parmesan cheese rind and a bay leaf if desired, cover and let simmer for about 3 hours
  9. Once done cooking, add in your heavy cream and a healthy sprinkle of parmesan cheese

For the mornay sauce:

  1. Melt butter in a pot
  2. Add in your garlic to allow to cook on direct heat for about 1 minute
  3. Whisk in flour until well incorporated
  4. Slowly add in your milk
  5. Simmer until thickened, enough to evenly coat the back of a spoon
  6. Remove from heat, stir in your parmesan cheese
  7. Season to taste with salt and pepper

Assembly:

  1. Preheat oven to 425
  2. In your dish that you plan to cook lasagna, begin by ladling a thin layer of bolognese at the bottom
  3. Add a layer of pasta
  4. Another layer of bolognese
  5. Add a layer of mornay
  6. Layer in some prosciutto, mozzarella, and basil
  7. Cover it all with pasta
  8. Repeat this process until all ingredients are used up or until you reach top of pan
  9. Make sure your top layer is your bolognese covered with your mornay sauce
  10. Top it all off with your shredded mozzarella cheese
  11. Bake for about an hour, until the pasta is cooked through and the cheese on top is browned and bubbly
  12. Enjoy!
