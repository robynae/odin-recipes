Butternut Squash Risotto Recipe

This risotto is perfect for chilly fall evenings.

As soon as the leaves begin to turn color, I rush to the grocery store to pick up the ingredients for this fall recipe. This rich risotto will be your new go-to!

Ingredients

For the risotto:

  • 1 cup arborio rice
  • 32 oz chicken stock
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 1-2 cups dry white wine
  • 1 8oz block parmesan cheese, grated

For the butternut puree:

  • 1 butternut squash, halved and deseeded
  • Sage leaves
  • 1 cup chicken stock
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Pepper

For the garnishments:

  • 6 tbsps butter
  • Sage leaves
  • Salt
  • Bacon
  • Mapple syrup

Cooking Instructions:

For the butternut puree:

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees
  2. Cover your halved butternut squash with olive oil, salt, and pepper
  3. Stuff the holes with sage leaves
  4. Turn your squash face down and roast for 45-60 minutes, or until completely soft throughout
  5. Remove the skin and sage leaves, and add roasted squash to a blender with chicken stock
  6. Blend until smooth

For the risotto:

  1. Begin heating your chicken stock in a small pot
  2. In a larger pot, begin sauteeing your onions in some olive oil until translucent
  3. Add in arborio rice, stirrin until edges begin to turn translucent
  4. Add in your white wine, stir until alcohol is cooked out
  5. Stirring constantly add in chicken stock one ladleful at a time, until fully absorbed
  6. Continue last step until arborio rice is cooked through, add in butternut puree and maple syrup
  7. Add in parmesan cheese and salt and pepper to taste

Garnishments:

  1. Cook bacon until crispy, dice into small bits
  2. Add sage and butter into a small saucepan, cook over low to medium heat until butter has browned
